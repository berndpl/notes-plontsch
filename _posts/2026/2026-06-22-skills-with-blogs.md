---
title: "Your Skill Might Need a Blog"
---

A skill that doesn't improve over time isn't really a skill, it's a static capability. The difference is whether it grows better the more you use it, whether it becomes part of you and your toolkit rather than just a tool you reach for until you don’t.

How? Your skill needs memory. A log, a blog, something that documents how you used it and what you learned. Trying things out. Noting what worked. What what didn’t work. Reflection.

There's a really nice side effect to this that's easy to miss: it's rewarding in a different way than most feedback loops. It's not just the slot-machine hit of reward at the moment of delivery. 

It's also the feeling of looking back and seeing the evolution, seeing how you improved, and knowing you had a hand in that.

So just tell the skill you use every day to keep a log of itself and be delighted checking in on it from time to time.
